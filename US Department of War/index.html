Air Superiority Fighters

Air Superiority Fighters are purpose-built combat aircraft designed to achieve and maintain control of the skies by defeating enemy aircraft in air-to-air combat. Their primary mission is to dominate aerial battlespace, clear air corridors, and ensure friendly forces can operate without threat from hostile aircraft. These fighters prioritize maneuverability, advanced sensors, superior weapon systems, and high-performance propulsion to outperform opponents in both beyond-visual-range (BVR) and within-visual-range (WVR) engagements.

Examples



F-22 Raptor

SU-57 Felon

J-20 Mighty Dragon

Multirole Fighters

Multirole fighters are highly adaptable combat aircraft engineered to perform a wide range of missions, allowing them to switch between air-to-air and air-to-ground roles with minimal reconfiguration. Their design emphasizes flexibility, enabling a single platform to conduct interception, ground attack, reconnaissance, electronic warfare, and close air support depending on mission needs. This versatility makes them extremely valuable to modern air forces seeking cost efficiency and operational flexibility.

Examples



F-35 Lightning

JAS 39E Gripen NG

Interceptors

Interceptors are high-speed fighter aircraft designed primarily to detect, engage, and destroy incoming enemy aircraft before they reach their targets. Their core mission is defensive: to quickly scramble, climb rapidly to high altitude, and neutralize threats such as strategic bombers, reconnaissance aircraft, or hostile fighters approaching national airspace.


Interceptors prioritize speed, climb rate, and long-range radar over multirole flexibility. Many are equipped with powerful engines, large radars, and long-range air-to-air missiles optimized for beyond-visual-range (BVR) combat. Because of their specialized mission, they typically carry lighter ground-attack capabilities or none at all.

Examples



MIG-31 Fox Bound

F-15c Eagle

SU-35s

Carrier-Based Fighters

Carrier-based fighters are combat aircraft engineered to operate from aircraft carriers, which requires unique structural and operational adaptations. These fighters feature reinforced landing gear, tailhooks for arrested landings, and folding wings to conserve deck and hangar space. Their design prioritizes versatility, ruggedness, and the ability to conduct missions far from traditional airbases.


Carrier-based fighters typically perform air superiority, fleet defense, strike missions, and reconnaissance, making them core assets of naval aviation. Their systems are optimized for maritime environments, including corrosion-resistant materials and advanced navigation for over-water operations.

Examples



F/A-18E/F Super Hornet

Dassault Rafale M

F-35 Lightning

Stealth Fighters

Stealth fighters focus on evading detection by radar and infrared sensors. They combine low-observable technology, advanced avionics, and precision-guided weaponry to penetrate heavily defended airspace safely. Stealth fighters excel in surprise attacks, deep-strike missions, and high-value target suppression, offering air forces the ability to operate with minimal exposure to enemy defenses.

Examples



F-117 Nighthawk

F-22 Raptor

F-35 Lightning

Stealth Fighters

Trainer fighters are specialized aircraft designed to prepare pilots for flying high-performance combat aircraft. They are used for advanced flight training, weapons practice, and tactical simulations before pilots transition to front-line fighters. These aircraft are typically slower, easier to handle, and more forgiving than operational fighters, while still incorporating many systems found in combat jets, such as ejection seats, avionics, and weapon simulation.


Modern trainer fighters often serve a dual role as light attack or reconnaissance aircraft, allowing them to contribute to missions while maintaining their primary purpose of pilot development.

Examples



BAE Hawk

T-50 Golden Eagle

Aermacchi M-346
